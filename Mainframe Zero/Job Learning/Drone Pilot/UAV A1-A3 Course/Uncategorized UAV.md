### What to do when your drone does not have the C designation? Are you allowed to fly it?

**Yes – you are allowed to fly it.** The absence of the designation most likely means that your drone was manufactured before 1 January 2024 or the manufacturer did not assign a class to your UAV.
Consult your instruction manual or designation on your drone to see the maximum take-off mass of your UAV, if:

1. The drone has the maximum take-off mass of less than 250 g:
    - the training which you have just started is not obligatory, but we strongly insist that you complete it – for your own safety and for the safety of other airspace users;
    - you may fly over uninvolved persons with particular caution and limiting the overflight time to the minimum;
    - you cannot fly over assemblies of people;
    - Operator's Manual: Carefully review the drone's operator's manual for detailed instructions and safety guidelines.
2. Drones 250g and above (but less than 25kg):
    - Maintain Distance: Maintain a safe distance from people;
    - 150m Horizontal Separation: Keep a horizontal distance of 150 meters from residential, commercial, industrial, or recreational areas;
    - Operator's Manual: Carefully review the drone's operator's manual for detailed instructions and safety guidelines;
    - Mandatory Training and Exam: Complete the provided training course and pass the final exam to obtain an A3 sub-category drone operator license.
3. The drone has take-off mass up to 900 g:  
    - you must complete this course and pass the final test – you will then be allowed to fly a drone in subcategory A1;
    - you may fly over uninvolved persons with particular caution and limiting the overflight time to the minimum;
    - you cannot fly over assemblies of people;
    - you must use a drone with a C1 class designation assigned by the manufacturer for the flight operation.
4. The drone has take-off mass up to 4 kg:  
    - you must complete this course and pass the test – you will then be allowed to fly a drone in subcategory A3;
    - you may fly over uninvolved persons with particular caution and limiting the overflight time to the minimum;
    - you cannot fly over assemblies of people;
    - if you need to fly within 50 metres [or lower] from uninvolved persons – complete the course and pass the exam for subcategory A2.
5. The drone has take-off mass up to 25 kg:  
    - you must complete this course and pass the test – you will then be allowed to fly your drone in subcategory A3;
    - you must maintain your UAV at a horizontal distance of at least 150 metres from residential, commercial, industrial or recreational areas;
    - you cannot fly over assemblies of people;

> [!abstract] Remember
>  Pursuant to EU regulations, an ‘assembly of people’ is when the density of people within a given area prevents their free movement (e.g., during a concert, mass).
