> **What to expect:** 
>- **2-Hour Coding Interview:**
> This coding challenge is crafted to evaluate your proficiency in data structures, coding skills, problem-solving acumen, time management, and effective technical communication. During this session, you will have the opportunity to demonstrate your ability to design and implement solutions independently. Subsequently, a comprehensive discussion will be held with the interviewer to delve into your design decisions, trade-offs made, and a thorough walkthrough of your code. We'll be focusing on your coding style, your understanding of data structures, and how you optimize for runtime and memory usage. Knowledge of big O notation, being able to discuss design tradeoffs, and familiarity with standard input and output streams (STDIN, STDOUT) in your language of choice will be useful.
> - **System Design:** 
> This interview is focused on understanding and evaluating your design abilities. You are given the task of coming up with the architecture for a system, which you have to build from scratch. You are expected to be comfortable coding parts of the solution on a white board, in the language of your choice. We expect you to be able to clarify and work complex problems and work through many complicated edge cases, communicate technically and work collaboratively, and finally convert concepts into actual code. Candidates should expect a non-standard system design interview – it’s not a high-level, whiteboard-style architecture discussion. Instead, it focuses more on designing from a coding perspective, and may include implementation aspects.
